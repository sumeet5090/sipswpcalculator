<h2 id="foundation">2. Secure Your Financial Foundation First</h2>
Do not start investing in high-risk equity markets until you have covered two defensive pillars:

*   **Pillar A: Emergency Fund:** Save at least 3 months of basic living expenses (around ₹50,000) in a liquid savings account or recurring deposit. If you face a sudden job loss or medical issue, this cash buffer stops you from selling your long-term mutual fund investments prematurely.
*   **Pillar B: Basic Health Insurance:** If your employer does not provide comprehensive health coverage, purchase a basic individual policy. A single major medical emergency can wipe out years of stock market gains.

<h2 id="compounding-power">3. The Power of a Step-Up SIP (The Math)</h2>
Let's look at the numbers. If you start a **modest SIP** of ₹5,000/month at age 25 and hold it for 30 years (until age 55), assuming a **12% average annual return** on equity:

*   **Flat SIP (₹5,000/mo):**
    *   Total Invested: ₹18.00 Lakh
    *   Total Maturity Value: **₹1.63 Crore**
*   **With a 10% Annual Step-Up:**
    If you increase your SIP amount by just **10% every year** to match your annual salary raises (from ₹5,000 to ₹5,500 in Year 2, and so on):
    *   Total Invested: ₹98.7 Lakh (gradually increasing as your income grows)
    *   Total Maturity Value: **₹3.68 Crore!**

By stepping up your contributions, you more than **double** your final wealth with minimal friction because the increment mirrors your salary growth.

<h2 id="allocation">4. Recommended Growth Asset Allocation</h2>
In your 20s, you have a long investment horizon (20+ years). You should focus on equity mutual funds for maximum compounding. A standard growth allocation looks like this:

1.  **50% in a Nifty 50 Index Fund:** Low-cost, passive exposure to the largest 50 companies in India.
2.  **30% in a Flexi-cap or Mid-cap Fund:** Access to mid-sized companies with strong growth potential.
3.  **20% in an Active Small-cap Fund:** High volatility, but adds significant growth over a 10+ year period.

---

<h2 id="mistakes">5. Pitfalls to Avoid in Your 20s</h2>
*   **Chasing "Hot Tips" or Day Trading:** 90%+ of retail day traders and options traders lose money. Focus on systematic, long-term compounding instead of trying to get rich quick.
*   **Waiting for a "Better Salary":** Do not wait until you earn more to start. A smaller SIP started at age 25 compounds more than a much larger SIP started at age 35. Start small immediately.
*   **Not Automating Your Savings:** Set your SIP auto-debit date to the day after your salary arrives. This forces you to invest first and spend what is left, rather than trying to save whatever is left at the end of the month.
