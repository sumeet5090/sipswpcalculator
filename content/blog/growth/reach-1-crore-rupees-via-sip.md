<h2 id="math-of-crore">The Math of a Crore: Why It's More Achievable Than You Think</h2>
<p>A crore rupees. It feels like an impossibly large number. But when you break it down into daily or monthly chunks, and let compound interest work in your favour, it becomes highly achievable — even on a modest salary.</p>

<p>Here's the compounding equation that makes it possible:</p>
<div class="bg-slate-900 border border-slate-700 text-emerald-400 p-6 rounded-xl font-mono text-center my-8 shadow-xl">
    Small Monthly Amount × Time × Compounding = Crore Plus Corpus
</div>

<p>At 12% average annual return (a realistic benchmark for equity mutual funds in India), your money doubles roughly every 6 years. This means your corpus accelerates dramatically in the later years: the first ₹10 lakh takes the longest, but the last ₹50 lakh of a ₹1 crore corpus comes shockingly quickly. Most people give up during the slow early phase and miss the explosive later phase.</p>

<h2 id="factors">Three Pillars to Accelerate Your Journey</h2>

### 1. Reinvest Dividend payouts (Growth Option)
Always choose the <strong>Direct-Growth Plan</strong> instead of IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal). Under the growth option, all gains are automatically reinvested back into the fund, maximizing the compounding velocity.

### 2. Set Up a Step-Up SIP (Top-Up)
As you get salary increments or business profits, automate an annual top-up of 10%. An extra ₹1,000/month added every year increases your maturity value by lakhs, matching your salary growth curve.

### 3. Maintain Asset Discipline
Do not redeem your equity mutual fund units during brief market corrections (e.g., 10-20% drops). These corrections are normal cycles. Redemptions trigger premature exit loads and lock in capital gains tax.
